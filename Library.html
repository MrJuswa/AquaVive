What role does the Philippine coral reefs play in our country?

Healthy coral reefs support artisanal and commercial fisheries. Coral reef fisheries such as groupers and lobsters directly rely on the reef for spawning and habitat.

Why are the coral reefs in decline?

Coral reefs are in the state of decline because of threats from local human activities such as overfishing and destructive fishing, coastal development, watershed-based pollution and marine-based pollution and damage . These threats have caused the decline in fisheries production for coral reefs in the Philippines since the 1970s.

How damaged are the coral reefs in the Philippines?

Oceana's recent study shows that 90% of our reefs are in poor to fair categories and none in excellent state . The dismal condition of our reefs is further exacerbated by man-made actions.

What factors contribute to the degredation of coral reefs?

The Philippines is home to 600 hard coral species. However, decades of pollution, destructive fishing, and overfishing have taken a toll. But climate change poses the greatest threat: record-breaking global temperatures, worsened by rising greenhouse gases, fuel ocean warming and acidification.

What are the Latest News about the Philippine Coral Reefs?

Dynamite Fishing

Illegal dynamite fishing is the use of explosives to stun or kill fish, causing environmental destruction and violating fishing laws.

Cyanide Fishing

Cyanide fishing is the illegal practice of using cyanide to stun fish for easy capture, harming marine ecosystems and coral reefs.

Trawling

Trawling is a fishing method that involves dragging a large net through the water or along the sea floor to catch fish, often causing habitat damage.

Longline

Longline fishing is a method where a long main line with baited hooks is set in the water to catch fish, often leading to bycatch of non-target species.

Ghost Fishing

Ghost fishing occurs when lost or abandoned fishing gear continues to trap and kill marine life unintentionally.

Overfishing

Overfishing is the excessive harvesting of fish from a body of water, depleting populations and disrupting marine ecosystems.
